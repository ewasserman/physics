The project is to develop a physical object simulation system built from scratch (not just a library we find). The objects modelable by the library should start simple with idealized representations (e.g. 4 discs with two zero-friction axles through a
rectangular prism makes a "car"). The goal is to be able to simulate a number of compound objects of this type, in an environment (e.g. floor, walls, buildings), allow for external forces (e.g. gravity, or a "nudge" by a user) to act on the objects, and for
the objects to interact with each other and the environment (cars bump into each other and the walls). The physics of the interactions will be idealized as is prudent (simple models of friction, elasticity, etc) and approximate (e.g. numerical
approximations). The idea is to produce something that looks realistic to an observer but efficient and speedy. The purpose of the model simulation is to produce data for training AI's to deal with physical objects. Therefore the model needs to produce at any
 instant the configuration of the system and its dynamical variables. What exactly these are is part of the design of the probject but the point is to be able to take observations of the real world (e.g. a video) and build a model from them with initial
values of the dynamical model and have the model be able to simulate what happens next.
